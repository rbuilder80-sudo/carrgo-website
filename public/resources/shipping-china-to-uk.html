Sea Freight from China to UK

Sea freight is the most popular and cost-effective method for shipping from China to the UK. Two options are available:

FCL (Full Container Load)

  • 20ft container: Holds approximately 28 cubic metres
  • 40ft container: Holds approximately 68 cubic metres
  • 40ft High Cube: Extra height for volumetric cargo
  • Transit time: 25-35 days port-to-port
  • Best for: Shipments over 15 cubic metres, regular importers

LCL (Less than Container Load)

  • Minimum: 1 cubic metre
  • Transit time: 28-40 days (includes consolidation/deconsolidation)
  • Best for: Smaller shipments, samples, trial orders

Air Freight from China to UK

Air freight is ideal for time-sensitive shipments. Major Chinese departure airports include Shanghai Pudong (PVG), Beijing Capital (PEK), Shenzhen (SZX), and Guangzhou (CAN). UK arrival airports include Heathrow (LHR), East Midlands (EMA), and Manchester (MAN).

  • Transit time: 3-7 days door-to-door
  • Best for: Urgent orders, high-value goods, electronics, samples
  • Cost: Significantly higher than sea freight (typically 8-15x more)

Rail Freight via the New Silk Road

The New Silk Road rail route connects China to the UK via Kazakhstan, Russia, Belarus, Poland, and Germany. Cargo arrives at London Gateway or East Midlands for UK distribution.

  • Transit time: 18-22 days
  • Cost: Midway between sea and air freight
  • Best for: Regular shipments where sea is too slow and air is too expensive
  • Route: Yiwu/Xi'an → Alashankou → Kazakhstan → Russia → Belarus → Poland → Germany → UK

Major Ports & Routes

Chinese ports: Shanghai, Ningbo, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Qingdao, Xiamen, Tianjin

UK ports: Felixstowe, Southampton, London Gateway, Liverpool, Tilbury

Most sea freight from China arrives at Felixstowe (UK's largest container port) or Southampton. Your freight forwarder will select the optimal route based on your supplier's location and final UK destination.

UK Customs Requirements

All goods imported from China to the UK require:

  • GB EORI number (obtain free from UK government)
  • Commercial invoice and packing list
  • Bill of lading or airway bill
  • Correct HS commodity codes for your products
  • Customs declaration via CDS (Customs Declaration Service)
  • Payment of import duty and VAT (if not deferred)

A freight forwarder handles all customs clearance documentation, ensuring compliance and preventing delays.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does shipping from China to the UK take?

Sea freight takes 25-35 days, rail freight 18-22 days, and air freight 3-7 days.

What is the cheapest way to ship from China to the UK?

Sea freight LCL is the cheapest option for small shipments, while FCL offers the best value for larger volumes.

Do I need a freight forwarder to import from China?

While not legally required, using a freight forwarder significantly simplifies the process, ensures customs compliance, and often secures better shipping rates through their carrier relationships.
