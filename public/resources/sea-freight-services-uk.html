FCL (Full Container Load)

FCL shipping means you book an entire shipping container exclusively for your cargo. It's the most economical choice for larger shipments.

When to choose FCL:

  • Your cargo exceeds 15-20 cubic metres
  • You ship regularly from the same supplier
  • You want faster transit (no consolidation delays)
  • Your goods are fragile or high-value (less handling)

LCL (Less than Container Load)

LCL shipping means your goods share a container with other shippers. You pay only for the space your cargo occupies.

When to choose LCL:

  • Your cargo is under 15 cubic metres
  • You're ordering samples or trial quantities
  • You want to test a new supplier
  • You ship infrequently

LCL considerations: LCL takes 3-7 days longer than FCL due to consolidation (gathering multiple shipments into one container) and deconsolidation (splitting at destination). Your goods are handled more frequently, increasing slight risk of damage.

Specialised Container Types

  • Refrigerated (Reefer): Temperature-controlled for food, pharmaceuticals
  • Open Top: For oversized cargo that can't fit through container doors
  • Flat Rack: For heavy machinery, vehicles, large equipment
  • Double Door (Tunnel): Doors at both ends for easy loading/unloading
  • Side Opening: Full side access for palletised goods

Major UK Container Ports

Felixstowe (Port of Felixstowe): UK's largest container port, handling 48% of containerised trade. Located in Suffolk with excellent rail connections to Midlands and North.

Southampton: Second largest container port, deep-water capability for largest vessels. Strong road and rail links.

London Gateway (DP World): Modern deep-sea port with Europe's largest logistics park. Automated operations.

Liverpool2: New deep-water container terminal serving the North of England and Ireland.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does sea freight to the UK take?

From China: 25-35 days. From India: 18-25 days. From USA East Coast: 10-14 days. From Europe: 2-7 days.

What's the difference between FCL and LCL?

FCL = you book a whole container. LCL = your goods share a container. FCL is cheaper per unit for large shipments; LCL is more economical for small shipments.

Can I track my sea freight shipment?

Yes. Carrgo provides tracking for all sea freight shipments, including container numbers, vessel names, and estimated arrival times.

What documents do I need for sea freight imports?

Commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading, EORI number, and correct HS commodity codes. Your freight forwarder prepares all documentation.
