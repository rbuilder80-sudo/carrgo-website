Cost Comparison

Cost is typically the deciding factor when choosing between air and sea freight. Sea freight is significantly cheaper for most shipments.

FactorAir FreightSea Freight
Price per kg3.00 - 8.00 GBP0.15 - 0.50 GBP
Price per CBM150 - 400 GBP30 - 80 GBP
Minimum charge50 - 100 GBP100 - 300 GBP
Fuel surchargeHigher, fluctuatesLower, more stable
Security feesIncluded in rateAdditional

Rule of thumb: Air freight costs 8-15 times more than sea freight per kilogram. For shipments under 100kg, the difference may be less dramatic due to sea freight minimum charges.

Cargo Suitability

When to Choose Air Freight:

  • High-value electronics and tech products
  • Perishable goods (food, flowers, pharmaceuticals)
  • Time-sensitive documents
  • Fashion and seasonal items
  • Emergency or replacement parts
  • Small, lightweight shipments
  • Products with high profit margins

When to Choose Sea Freight:

  • Bulk commodities and raw materials
  • Furniture and home goods
  • Heavy machinery and equipment
  • Non-perishable consumer goods
  • Large volume shipments
  • Regular, planned inventory restocking
  • Cost-sensitive products
Prohibited/Restricted in Air Freight: Certain items cannot be shipped by air due to IATA regulations, including flammable liquids, explosives, certain chemicals, and lithium batteries (with restrictions). Sea freight has fewer restrictions on hazardous materials.

Environmental Impact

Sea freight has a significantly lower carbon footprint than air freight:

  • Air freight: ~500g CO2 per tonne-km
  • Sea freight: ~10-40g CO2 per tonne-km

For environmentally conscious businesses, sea freight is 12-50 times more carbon-efficient. Some companies offset air freight emissions through carbon credit programs.

Decision Framework

Use this simple framework to choose your shipping method:

  1. Is it urgent? (needed within 2 weeks) → Air freight
  2. Is it heavy? (over 500kg or 2 CBM) → Sea freight
  3. Is it valuable? (over 50 GBP/kg) → Consider air freight
  4. Is it perishable? → Air freight
  5. Is cost the main factor? → Sea freight
  6. Is it hazardous? → Likely sea freight

Hybrid Solutions

Many businesses use a combination of both methods:

  • Split shipments: Send urgent items by air, bulk by sea
  • Seasonal strategy: Air freight for peak season, sea for baseline inventory
  • Product tiering: High-margin products by air, low-margin by sea
  • Air-to-sea conversion: Start with air freight for new products, switch to sea once demand is proven
