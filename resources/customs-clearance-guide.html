What Is Customs Clearance?

Customs clearance is the process of getting goods approved by customs authorities to enter or leave a country. In the UK, this involves submitting a customs declaration to HMRC, paying any applicable duties and taxes, and ensuring all documentation is correct and complete.

Since Brexit, all goods imported into the UK from the EU and the rest of the world must go through customs clearance. This has made the role of customs clearance agents more important than ever for UK businesses.

EORI Number Requirements

An EORI number (Economic Operators Registration and Identification) is mandatory for all businesses importing goods into the UK. It starts with GB followed by 12 digits.

Important: Without a GB EORI number, your goods cannot clear UK customs. Apply for free at the UK government website before arranging any shipments.

To apply:

  1. Visit the UK government's EORI registration page
  2. Have your VAT number (if registered) and company details ready
  3. Applications are typically processed within 3-5 working days

HS Commodity Codes

Every product imported into the UK must be classified with a Harmonised System (HS) commodity code. This 6-10 digit code determines:

  • The rate of import duty
  • Whether import licences or restrictions apply
  • Whether preferential duty rates are available under trade agreements

You can find your commodity code using the UK government's Trade Tariff tool. If you're unsure, a customs clearance agent can classify your goods correctly.

Required Documents

For customs clearance in the UK, you typically need:

  1. Commercial Invoice: Shows the value, description, and origin of goods
  2. Packing List: Details contents of each package, weights, and dimensions
  3. Bill of Lading (sea) or Airway Bill (air): Transport document from the carrier
  4. EORI number: Your GB EORI
  5. Commodity codes: HS codes for all products
  6. Country of origin certificate: May qualify for preferential duty rates
  7. Import licence: Required for restricted goods only

Customs Declaration Service (CDS)

The UK uses the Customs Declaration Service (CDS) for all import declarations. This replaced the previous CHIEF system. All customs declarations must be submitted electronically through CDS, either by the importer or by a customs agent acting on their behalf.

There are two main types of declarations:

  • Full declaration: Submitted before goods arrive, with full duty payment
  • Simplified declaration: Allows goods to be released with minimal information, with full declaration submitted later

Import Duty & VAT

When importing goods into the UK, you may need to pay:

  • Import duty: Calculated as a percentage of the goods' value (determined by HS code and country of origin)
  • Import VAT: 20% of the total value including goods, duty, shipping, and insurance

Example calculation for goods worth 10,000 GBP from China:

  • Goods value: 10,000
  • Import duty (5%): 500
  • Shipping + insurance: 1,500
  • VATable value: 12,000
  • Import VAT (20%): 2,400
  • Total import costs: 2,900

Customs Clearance Agents

A customs clearance agent (also called a customs broker) handles all aspects of customs clearance on your behalf. They:

  • Prepare and submit customs declarations
  • Ensure correct HS code classification
  • Calculate and arrange payment of duties and taxes
  • Liaise with HMRC if queries arise
  • Keep records for the required 4+ years
  • Advise on duty reliefs and trade agreements

Most UK freight forwarders offer customs clearance as part of their service, providing a single point of contact for your entire shipment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does UK customs clearance take?

For straightforward shipments with correct documentation, customs clearance typically takes 2-24 hours. Delays occur if documentation is incorrect or if HMRC selects the shipment for inspection.

Can I clear customs myself without an agent?

Yes, but you need access to the CDS system and knowledge of customs procedures. Most businesses use a customs agent to ensure compliance and avoid costly mistakes.

What happens if my goods are held at customs?

Goods may be held for document checks, physical inspection, or duty queries. Your customs agent will liaise with HMRC to resolve any issues. Storage fees may apply at the port during delays.

Do I still need customs clearance for EU goods post-Brexit?

Yes. Since 1 January 2021, all goods imported from the EU to the UK require full customs clearance, just like goods from non-EU countries.
