UK Customs Clearance Explained for Ecommerce Importers

Customs clearance is the step between your shipment arriving and it reaching your warehouse. Learn what happens, what you need, and how to avoid delays.

Get a Free Quote Customs Clearance Service

What Is Customs Clearance?

Customs clearance is the process of getting your goods approved by UK customs (HMRC) so they can enter the country legally. It involves:

  • Declaring what the goods are
  • Paying any duty and VAT owed
  • Presenting the correct paperwork
  • Passing any inspections

Until clearance is complete, your goods sit in a port or airport customs zone.

Who Handles Customs Clearance?

You have three options:

OptionHow It WorksBest For
Direct to HMRCYou make the declaration yourself using CHIEF or CDSLarge importers with in-house customs teams
Customs brokerA specialist makes the declaration on your behalfMost SMEs
Freight forwarderYour forwarder (like Carrgo) handles transport + clearanceImporters who want one point of contact

What Paperwork Do You Need?

  1. Commercial Invoice — Shows what you're buying, from whom, and for how much.
  2. Packing List — Lists what's in each box, weight and dimensions.
  3. Bill of Lading / Air Waybill — Proof of shipment from the carrier.
  4. EORI Number — Your UK business import ID. How to get one.
  5. Commodity Code — The HS code for your product. How to find it.
  6. Certificate of Origin — Sometimes required for preferential duty rates.
  7. Import Licence — Required for restricted goods (food, chemicals, electronics, etc.).

The Customs Clearance Process Step by Step

Step 1: Arrival. Your shipment arrives at a UK port or airport.

Step 2: Pre-clearance (optional). A good forwarder submits the declaration before the goods arrive, speeding things up.

Step 3: Declaration submitted. Your broker or forwarder submits the customs declaration via CDS (Customs Declaration Service).

Step 4: Duty and VAT payment. If not using Postponed VAT Accounting, duty and VAT are paid at this stage.

Step 5: Customs check (if selected). HMRC may inspect the shipment. This is random or risk-based.

Step 6: Release. Once cleared, the goods are released for delivery to your warehouse.

How Long Does Customs Clearance Take?

ScenarioTypical Time
Standard sea freight, no inspection1–2 days
Air freight, no inspectionSame day or next day
Selected for inspection+2–5 days
Missing paperworkDelayed until resolved

Common Causes of Customs Delays

ProblemSolution
Wrong commodity codeDouble-check before shipping
Missing commercial invoiceAlways include a detailed invoice
EORI not providedApply before you import
Duty/VAT unpaidSet up PVA or ensure funds available
Restricted goods without licenceCheck licensing requirements first

Customs Clearance Costs

  • Customs declaration fee: £25–£75 per declaration (varies by broker).
  • Inspection fee: £50–£150 if HMRC opens your container.
  • Port storage: Charged if goods sit too long before clearance.

Tips for Smooth Clearance

  1. Use a reputable forwarder who submits declarations early.
  2. Get your paperwork right the first time — don't guess at codes or values.
  3. Be accurate on your commercial invoice — under-declaring value is fraud.
  4. Check if your goods need a licence before you ship.
  5. Ask about pre-clearance — it can save days.
